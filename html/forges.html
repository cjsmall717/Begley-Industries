Burners and Refractories, Forges and Furnaces

  • Burners

    Burners were assembled from readily available, cheap components. My early design was based on Ron Weil's work. It's still in use - I use it in my furnace which was the first project I undertook and was constructed in an old 20L paint can lined with a home-made refractory cement. In order to test the burner, independent of the furnace body, the end of the burner had a makeshift flare made from some copper sheet.

    In each case, jets were fashioned from MIG welding tips rebored to the correct diameter. Where parts were soldered, a high-content silver solder was used in order to avoid melting. Drilled and tapped parts (jets) were silver soldered in order to avoid mishap.

    After some time, the cast iron flares that were used on later burners were subject to heavy oxidation and melting so these were replaced with flared steel pipe, and held in place with three grub screws offset at 120 degrees to each other.

  • Refractories

    Commercial refractories were chosen for this build. Duracast castable refractory and kaowool were sourced from 'Artisan Supplies'. After the kaowool lining was installed, it was fixed with a setting agent (presumably sodium silicate solution with colouring).

  • Forges

    Welding is an artform in itself - one that I do not pretend to have mastered. In spite of that, it's holding up.

    Reclaimed exhaust pipe was used for the appertures for the burners because the alloy is resistant to corrosion and heat. After the burners were installed, kaowool was packed around them to reduce the amount of heat radiated or convected back towards the intake.
