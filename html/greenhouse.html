Greenhouse

Designed and built a greenhouse, making use of reclaimed materials, and new materials as required, in an effort to extend the growing season - Canberra winters are bitterly cold and the summers are hot and dry—as a consequence, growing seasons are short.

Overview

Designed and constructed a small-scale greenhouse (~3000x2000mm) and a heating system to support controlled plant growth using low-cost and reclaimed materials.

The system integrates passive solar gain, thermal mass, moisture retention, and a simple combustion-based heat source to extend growing conditions beyond ambient limits.

Key Features

  • Steeply sloping roof to optimise winter light path and water-runoff.
  • A sunken walkway serves as both a cold sink, and provides reaonable 'head-space'.
  • Rear wall was constructed from 12-ply; lower half packed with plastic-wrapped soil as thermal mass.
  • Planter boxes low down towards rear - maximising sun exposure.
  • Shelf for smaller pots for starting seedlings.
  • Rocket heater with thermal mass after-burner (see dedicated section).

Heating System

A simple rocket heater was integrated to provide supplementary heat during cold periods. The system was designed for efficiency and paired with thermal mass to retain heat.
