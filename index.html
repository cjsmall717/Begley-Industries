Beagley Industries

Who are you?

Systems and platform engineer experienced in high-performance, large-scale environments, with a foundation in biology and chemistry including biosynthetic pathway elucidation and the synthesis and characterisation of conducting polymer—hydrogel systems.

Proven ability to design, build, and troubleshoot complex systems across software, hardware, and experimental domains, particularly in environments where reliability, scale, constraints, and cost are real—not theoretical.

My work focuses on understanding systems end-to-end, identifying constraints, and building solutions that hold up under real-world use.

In addition to formal roles, I have undertaken a range of independent and ad-hoc projects spanning materials, instrumentation, and system design. This portfolio captures that broader body of work.

I also fabricate tools and systems from first principles, often using salvaged materials. This reflects a consistent approach: start with the problem, use what is available, minimise waste, and iterate toward a robust solution.

Inspirations

Beagley Industries was inspired by a number of things. It came on the tail, as it were, of a little, seventeen year old dog. The name, `Small', was Anglicised from the Irish, "O' Beaglaoich". Although my dog is not a beagle, I'd used my sketch of her for my beer label, and she is quite small. The label was never intended for broad dissemination and was intended more as a family joke...it was too good an inspiration for a brand or logo to let it go unused.
