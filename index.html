  • Who are you?

    I'm a systems and platform engineer with a background in high-performance and large-scale environments.

    Most of my work has been in contexts where reliability, scale, and constraints are real - not theoretical. That experience shapes how I approach problems: understand the system, respect the limits, and build solutions that hold up under use.

    Alongside that, I've worked on a range of smaller and more ad-hoc projects. They don't always fit neatly under a single role, but they reflect the same approach - practical, deliberate, and focused on making things work properly.

    I've consistently delivered strong outcomes in the environments I've worked in, including formal performance recognition. This portfolio exists to show the broader range of that work, beyond what a job title alone captures.

    If everything is treated as equally important, nothing really is. I focus on the problems that justify the effort, and see them through to a result.

    I also fabricate objects and systems built from first principles and, where possible, salvaged materials. Many projects draw on leftover materials from earlier work. This reflects a consistent approach: start with an idea; source or recover suitable materials; and aim to minimise waste through reuse. There is immense satisfaction in being able to fabricate a tool, to build a complex system and iteratively improve it, and to use materials that would otherwise find their way into land-fill.

Beagley Industries was inspired by a number of things. It came on the tail, as it were, of a little, seventeen year old dog. The name, `Small', was Anglicised from the Irish, "O' Beaglaoich". Although my dog is not a beagle, I'd used my sketch of her for my beer label, and she is quite small. The label was never intended for broad dissemination and was intended more as a family joke...it was too good an inspiration for a brand or logo to let it go unused.
